The setting is an extension of Richard himself. Plano is dry, void of life, depressive and understated вҖ” just like him. Then Hampden arrives: green, lush, full of opportunity. That first scene where he describes the euphoria of deep greens and swollen apples on the ground. Makes you happy for him.

Richard is essentially a bystander letting life happen to him, observing beauty by osmosis. Which makes it ironic that he gets framed as heroic in Henry's suicide. He found what he was looking for вҖ” the beauty вҖ” but the darkness came with it, and he kept digging deeper.

Henry was admirable because he really believed in all of it and followed through. Every other character was tainted by some observable deficiency, all trying to hide something. Henry just was.

Richard's words with Camilla on the train platform broke my heart. You watch something unavoidable and painful happen, like an animal falling into a trap.

VERDICT
Good book for winter.
