Super clever and hilarious. Corny in a wonderful way, but still corny. The humor and the Crowley/Aziraphale dynamic are what make it sing.

Was reading an interview Terry Pratchett and Neil Gaiman gave together on the book, and they talked about how fun it was to write it and how they were kind of competitive with one another. They also talked about how you can sometimes say really true things with humor that you couldn't otherwise say directly. I think that's because humor brings your guard down and lets all that other stuff in with the joke.

This book was super clever and hilarious, but there were also some cutting insights. The best two in my mind are first the one on the cosmic card game God seems to be playing with us all — a scary thought which has a lot of logic behind it. It's hard to know who to believe, what's true, what the stakes are. And second, the idea that humans are just human. Which is a pretty unifying thing. Even satanists aren't satanists 24/7 — most of the time they're doing their normal human stuff, like the God-fearing folk. Satanism is just something they do on Sundays.

Crowley and Aziraphale falling in love with the humans also made it very emotionally charged, as they were able to point out the great things about humanity while still acknowledging the ways we torture each other, all because of agency. So the end of the book kinda says: you're a human, you have these choices to make which even seem to influence the ineffable plan — what are you going to do about it?

VERDICT
Recommend to everyone. Funny enough to read in one sitting, sharp enough to think about for a while after.
